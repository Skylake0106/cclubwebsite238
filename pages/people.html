GVSU Computing Club

People

From Professors to grad-students, the level of expertise in those the Computing Club gets to work alongside is fundamental. Here's just a few of the people & groups that have taken the time to speak with & educate the Computing Club.

Erik Fredericks

Professor Erik Fredericks is an Assistant Professor in the GVSU School of Computing, teaching courses including (but not limited to) Software Engineering, Computer Graphics, and System Analysis & Design.

His diverse and numerous research fields are what he discussed when speaking to the Computing Club, highlighting his interests in graphical environments, video games, search-based-software engineering, self-adaptive systems, and beyond. Prof. Fredericks often does research alongside students and had brought one of his undergraduate researchers with him to help discuss one of their current projects concerning evolutionary test cases for testing power-consumption.

Christian Trefftz

Professor Christian Trefftz has been a Professor in the School of Computing and Information Systems here at GVSU since 2001, teaching courses with a focus on Computer Science, Data Structures and Algorithms, and much more.

His current research interest in parallel processing is what he discussed when he met with the Computing Club, talking about the history of it and the implementation of it in supercomputers and GPU processing. Prof. Trefftz also took time to describe his experience throughout his research and the opportunities he's had to work with other professors/students.

Graduate College

GVSU's Graduate School provides the environment and support that GV graduates need to prepare to enter their careers past their educations. They offer a plethora of graduate programs with the goal of refining student connections, opportunities, and guidance.

On March 31st, they gave a presentation for the Computing Club detailing some specifics of the 4 currently available CIS masters programs, emphasized/focused programs, and accelerated masters programs.
